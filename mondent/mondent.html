Dental Implants

Dental implants are artificial tooth roots that are surgically placed into the jawbone to support a replacement tooth or bridge. They are made of biocompatible materials such as titanium, and provide a strong and stable foundation for a natural-looking and functioning tooth restoration.

Restorative Dentistry

Restorative dentistry involves restoring damaged, decayed or missing teeth to their original function and appearance. This can include a range of treatments such as fillings, crowns, bridges, dentures, and dental implants. The aim is to improve the health, function, and aesthetics of a patient's teeth and gums, while also preventing further damage or decay.

Minor and Major Surgeries

Minor dental surgeries are simple procedures that can be performed in a dental office, such as tooth extractions, gum surgeries, or root canal therapy. Major dental surgeries are more complex procedures that may require hospitalization or general anesthesia, such as orthognathic surgery, jaw reconstruction, or full mouth rehabilitation.
